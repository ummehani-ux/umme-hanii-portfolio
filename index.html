Case study 01 / 05

DealHub AI website redesign

Role · UX/UI Designer Type · B2B SaaS, web Scope · 5 breakpoints

Overview

DealHub AI is a B2B "quote-to-revenue" SaaS platform. The marketing site had inconsistent UI patterns and a confusing nav hierarchy that was quietly costing them visitors. I ran a full redesign: heuristic audit → competitive analysis → wireframes → component library → responsive prototype.

The problem

  • Weak visual hierarchy — CTAs and proof competed for attention with no clear reading path
  • Inconsistent components — buttons and cards shifted shape section to section
  • Fragmented navigation — groupings didn't match how buyers think about the product

The solution

  • Rebuilt the IA so each section builds a case: trust → capability → proof → objection handling → CTA
  • Built a Figma component library — buttons, testimonial cards, feature tiles — with Auto Layout & Variants
  • Wireframed low-fi first, then tested content density across 5 breakpoints in high fidelity
OutcomeA component-driven system the DealHub team can extend — new integrations or testimonials slot in without breaking layout.
Case study 02 / 05

BigBite food delivery app

Role · UX/UI Designer Type · Mobile, iOS/Android Scope · 3 personas · 18+ screens

Overview

A mobile-first ordering app designed to feel fast and craveable rather than transactional. I designed 3 personas and 4 end-to-end journeys — discovery, browse, item detail, checkout — from low-fi wireframes to a linked high-fidelity prototype.

The problem

  • Checkout drop-off caused by unclear navigation — users lost track of where they were in the order flow
  • Category browsing felt disconnected from the cart

The solution

  • Persistent bottom nav keeps users oriented at every step
  • Search surfaces trending picks to cut typing and speed up discovery
  • Item detail leads with rating, calories, and prep time — the 3 things people actually decide on
  • Checkout condensed to one screen: address, payment, and price breakdown together
OutcomePricing stays transparent at every step and browsing is organized around cravings, not menus — directly targeting the drop-off the old flow caused.
Case study 03 / 05

MediTrack medicine tracking app

Role · UX/UI Designer Type · Mobile, healthcare Scope · WCAG 2.1 · 44px targets

Overview

A medication tracker for patients and caregivers, where a missed dose isn't just bad UX — it's a health risk. I mapped 2 personas and 4 pain points into reminder, dosage-tracking, and history screens, designed accessibility-first from the ground up.

The problem

  • Missed or duplicate doses are a real risk across multi-medication schedules
  • Caregivers need visibility without it feeling like surveillance
  • Needed to work for users with reduced dexterity or low vision

The solution

  • Calendar-first home view — "what's due today" is always the first thing seen
  • Purpose-driven onboarding tailors the experience from step one
  • WCAG 2.1 compliant — 44px+ touch targets, high-contrast states throughout
OutcomeThe core loop — what's due, did I take it — takes one tap to answer, for both patients and the caregivers supporting them.
Case study 04 / 05

NexPay personal finance app

Role · UX/UI Designer Type · Mobile, fintech Scope · 15+ screens

Overview

A budgeting app built to give clear, immediate visibility into where money is going. I drew on my B.Com background to design onboarding, budgeting, and categorization — using progressive disclosure to keep a data-heavy app feeling simple.

The problem

  • Finance apps tend to overwhelm with numbers or oversimplify past the point of usefulness
  • Poor visibility into spending patterns at a glance

The solution

  • Dashboard leads with Total Balance and a one-tap privacy toggle
  • Circular progress ring for budget — an instant visual read, no mental math
  • 12-category icon grid speeds up logging via recognition, not recall
  • Monthly trend line lets patterns surface without a spreadsheet
OutcomeColor-coded categories and progressive disclosure do the cognitive work — "where did my money go" becomes a glance, not a spreadsheet session.
Case study 05 / 06

Digital Agency landing page

Role · UX/UI Designer Type · Web, landing page Scope · F-pattern layout

Overview

A conversion-focused landing page for a digital product agency, built to turn cold visitors into project inquiries — using an F-pattern layout, an above-the-fold CTA strategy, and typographic contrast to guide the eye.

The problem

  • Agency pages often try to say everything at once and end up saying nothing clearly
  • Needed to be scannable in under 10 seconds by a first-time visitor

The solution

  • A single repeated CTA — "Start Your Project" — anchors hero, gallery, and footer
  • Masonry-style project grid with consistent "View Project" affordances
  • Typography carries hierarchy instead of boxes or color — bold display against a lighter support line
OutcomeEvery downstream section reinforces the same single call to action instead of introducing new decisions for the visitor to make.
Case study 06 / 06

Provio candidate verification platform

Role · UX/UI Designer Type · B2B SaaS, web Scope · 8-step verification flow + recruiter dashboard

Overview

Provio verifies candidates before companies hire them — AI-powered analysis of resumes, skills, portfolios, and references, delivering a trusted score recruiters can act on immediately. I designed both sides of the product: the candidate verification journey and the recruiter-facing dashboard.

The problem

  • Resume fraud routinely goes undetected in traditional hiring
  • Manual screening takes 3-5 weeks per candidate
  • Skills get claimed on a resume but never actually verified

The solution

  • An 8-step guided verification flow — resume, skills, identity, portfolio, references — each with live progress and a "% verified" readout
  • A recruiter dashboard surfacing a single trusted Provio Score plus role-fit match %, so decisions don't require re-checking the source data
  • A feature system spanning AI resume analysis, proctored skill tests, KYC identity checks, and structured reference outreach
OutcomeFull verification in under 24 hours instead of 3-5 weeks, with an objective, auditable score recruiters can act on the same day.
